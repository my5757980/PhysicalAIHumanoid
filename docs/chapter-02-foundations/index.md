# Chapter 2: Foundations of Humanoid Robotics

**Weeks 2-3 | Module 1: ROS 2 Foundations**

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

- Describe humanoid robot anatomy and degrees of freedom
- Explain forward and inverse kinematics concepts
- Identify common sensors used in humanoid robots
- Understand actuator types and their characteristics
- Calculate basic kinematic equations for robot limbs

## Chapter Contents

1. [Humanoid Anatomy](./anatomy) - Body structure and degrees of freedom
2. [Kinematics Fundamentals](./kinematics) - Motion mathematics
3. [Sensors Overview](./sensors) - Perceiving the world
4. [Actuators & Motors](./actuators) - Creating movement
5. [Power Systems](./power-systems) - Energy management in robots

## Key Concepts

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Degrees of Freedom (DoF) | Number of independent movements a robot can make |
| Forward Kinematics | Computing end-effector position from joint angles |
| Inverse Kinematics | Computing joint angles from desired end-effector position |
| Proprioception | Robot's sense of its own body position |

## Prerequisites

- Chapter 1 completed
- Basic linear algebra (vectors, matrices)
- Understanding of trigonometry

## Estimated Time

**10-12 hours** of study and hands-on exercises
